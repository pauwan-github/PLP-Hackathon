Flu Signs & Symptoms

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Sore throat
  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Headaches
  • Fatigue (tiredness)
  • Sometimes vomiting and diarrhea (more common in children)

Cause & Spread of Flu

  • Cause: Influenza viruses (types A, B, and C).
  • Person-to-person: Through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks.
  • Surface contact: Touching surfaces contaminated with the virus and then touching the face (mouth, nose, or eyes).

Prevention & Treatment of Flu

  • Prevention Methods:
    • Get an annual flu vaccine.
    • Wash hands frequently with soap and water.
    • Use alcohol-based hand sanitizers.
    • Avoid close contact with sick individuals.
    • Cover mouth and nose with a tissue or elbow when coughing or sneezing.
    • Disinfect commonly touched surfaces.
    • Maintain a healthy lifestyle to strengthen immunity (balanced diet, regular
      • exercise, adequate sleep).
  • Cure and Treatment
    • Rest and stay hydrated.
    • Over-the-counter medications to relieve symptoms (e.g., acetaminophen for fever, decongestants for nasal congestion).
    • Antiviral drugs (e.g., oseltamivir or zanamivir) may be prescribed, especially if started within 48 hours of symptom onset.
