Migraine Signs & Symptoms

  • Primary symptom
    • Moderate to severe headache, usually on one side of the head, throbbing or pulsing in nature.
  • Associated symptoms:
    • Nausea or vomiting
    • Sensitivity to light, sound, or smells
    • Aura: Visual disturbances (flashes of light, zigzag patterns, blind spots) before or during the headache
    • Fatigue and irritability

    Cause & Spread of Migraine

    • Causes
      • Exact cause is unclear but involves abnormal brain activity affecting nerve signals and blood flow.
    • Common triggers:
      • Stress or anxiety
      • Hormonal changes (e.g., menstrual cycle)
      • Certain foods (e.g., caffeine, alcohol, processed foods)
      • Lack of sleep or changes in sleep patterns
      • Strong sensory stimuli (bright lights, loud noises, strong smells)

    Prevention & Treatment of Migraine

    • Prevention Methods:
      • Identify and avoid triggers (keep a migraine diary).
      • Maintain a regular sleep schedule and manage stress.
      • Follow a healthy diet and stay hydrated.
      • Consider preventive medications (as prescribed by a doctor).
      • Use relaxation techniques like yoga or meditation.
    • Cure and Treatment:
      • Pain relief:
        • Over-the-counter pain relievers (e.g., ibuprofen, acetaminophen) for mild migraines.
        • Prescription medications like triptans (e.g., sumatriptan) for severe migraines.
      • Preventive medications (for frequent migraines):
        • Beta-blockers (e.g., propranolol)
        • Antidepressants (e.g., amitriptyline)
        • Anti-seizure drugs (e.g., topiramate)
        • CGRP inhibitors (e.g., erenumab)
        • Rest in a quiet, dark room during an episode.
        • Apply a cold or warm compress to the head or neck.
